# iLID/SspB Evidence

## Current State

This dossier is curator-promoted from two source-backed packets: the 2014 engineering paper and a 2015 comparative dimers guide.

## Source-Backed Claims

- The 2014 founding paper describes `iLID` as an improved light-inducible dimer created by embedding the bacterial `SsrA` peptide in the C-terminal helix of the `AsLOV2` domain from *Avena sativa*.
- The same paper reports a dark-state steric block that is released by blue-light-driven helix undocking, enabling `SspB` binding.
- The founding paper reports an over-50-fold light-dependent affinity change for `SspB`.
- The founding paper also reports functional demonstrations in mammalian cell culture for light-mediated localization and reversible small-GTPase signaling control.
- The 2015 comparative guide includes `iLID/SspB` among blue-light-inducible dimers whose dark-state and lit-state affinities correlate with cellular assay behavior.
