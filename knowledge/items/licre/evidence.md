# LiCre Evidence

## Current State

This dossier is currently anchored by the founding 2021 eLife paper.

## Source-Backed Claims

- `LiCre` is presented as a novel light-inducible Cre recombinase.
- The founding paper describes it as a single-chain protein containing the `AsLOV2` photoreceptor domain fused to a destabilized Cre variant.
- The paper reports activation within minutes under blue light and no need for added chemical cofactor.
- The abstract reports stronger and faster activation plus lower dark residual activity than unnamed split photoactivatable Cre systems.
- The same abstract reports functional use in yeast, including light-controlled `beta-carotene` production, and in human cells.

## Ranked Citations

1. Foundational: 2021 eLife, "A single-chain and fast-responding light-inducible Cre recombinase as a novel optogenetic switch" (`10.7554/eLife.61268`)

## Evidence Boundaries

- The current dossier slice is abstract-level rather than full-text.
- The abstract does not identify the exact comparator systems.
- The human-cell context is underspecified and should not be over-interpreted beyond a mammalian-cell use claim.
