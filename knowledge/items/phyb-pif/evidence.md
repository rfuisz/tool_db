# PhyB/PIF Evidence

## Current State

This dossier is intentionally partial. The current curated evidence comes from a mammalian-cell enablement paper rather than from the original switch-defining paper.

## Source-Backed Claims

- The 2017 PNAS paper identifies `PhyB/PIF` as the available red/far-red light-inducible dimerizer in scope for the study.
- The paper reports an expression vector that coexpresses `HO1`, `PcyA`, ferredoxin, and ferredoxin-NADP+ reductase to synthesize PCB in mammalian-cell mitochondria.
- The abstract reports that this PCB-synthesis system, together with `PhyB/PIF`, allowed optogenetic regulation of intracellular signaling without external chromophore supply.
- The same abstract reports that biliverdin reductase A depletion further increased intracellular PCB concentration.

## Ranked Citations

1. Mammalian enablement: 2017 PNAS, "Efficient synthesis of phycocyanobilin in mammalian cells for optogenetic control of cell signaling" (`10.1073/pnas.1707190114`)

## Evidence Boundaries

- This is not yet a complete origin dossier for `PhyB/PIF`.
- The abstract-level evidence is strong for mammalian chromophore enablement but weaker for canonical origin and broad benchmarking.
- Exact signaling pathway, cell line, and quantitative performance metrics are not yet captured here.
